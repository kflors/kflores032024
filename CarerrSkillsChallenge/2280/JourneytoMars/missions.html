Missions

The Mars Exploration Program is committed to unlocking the mysteries of the Red Planet by deploying a sophisticated fleet of robotic orbiters, landers, and rovers. These advanced systems work together, transmitting invaluable scientific data through a high-speed Mars-Earth communications network. This program, driven by cutting-edge technology and scientific curiosity, seeks to:

  • Investigate the origins and early development of Mars as a planetary body.
  • Examine the geological and climatic changes that have shaped its surface over time.
  • Explore the possibility that Mars once supported—or may still support—life.
  • Lay the groundwork for future human missions and deepen our understanding of how Mars compares to Earth.

By studying Mars, we gain critical insights into our solar system’s history and take significant steps toward the future of interplanetary exploration.
